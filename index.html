Welcome to my official website. This site exists because in 2006, I attempted to buy zaide.com. Its owner wanted $3000 for it, so I bought this domain instead. Several years later, I decided to use it. In 2014, I redid it into its current form, primarily to open source research and work related interests.

About Me

Unstructured Meshing

Currently a researcher at RPI as part of SCOREC. Previously, I was at the University of British Columbia, researching adaptive unstructured meshing for the simulation of semi-conductor manufacturing. I dream about triangles and tetrahedra and write software to realize those dreams.

Numerical Shockwaves

Previously, I completed my graduate studies at the University of Michigan under Professor Phil Roe researching numerical shock structure in shock-capturing schemes. I focused specifically on anomalous behavior and large errors produced by numerical shockwaves in shock capturing schemes.
